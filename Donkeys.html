    Overview

    Donkeys are a type of big farm animals. They are mostly used for their ability to work for long hours. They are one of the farm animals which are closely related to horses and mules

    Diet & Habitat

    Horses are herbivores and can eat most plant based foods including oliv oil but they should not be fed any dairy or baked products, onions, garlic, aubergines, potatoes, tomatoes or leeks. They should be provided with a lot of straw and hay and shoudl be fed at least 2 to 3 times a day. Donkeys require an indoor shelter to protect form cold weather and a large outdoor area for exercise and grazing.

    Threats & Diseases

    Donkeys prone to falling sick easily so it best to get them checked every few months for the following diseases:

    • Toothaches can occur when there is a cavity or infection in the mouth, this can prevent them from chewing properly. To avoid this the donkey's teeth should be cleaned after every meal.
    • When a donkey is overweight it can develop a hyperlipamia; a disease causes excess fat in the blood and lead to organ failiure. They may put on excess weight due to stress, lonliness or some other sudden changes in the environment. To help them it is good to take them on longer walks and changing their diet.
    • Infected hooves are caused due to stones and dirt getting stuck in them, they should be cleaned and trimmed regularly to prevent this.

    Donkeys also need regular brushing it is necessary to remove the dead skin cells and parasites.

    Fun Facts:

    • Donkeys can hear sounds 90 kilometers away.
    • If a donkey brays it means they are hungry or uncomfortable.
    • They can remember something from 25 years ago.
    • There are less than 200 white donkeys in the world.

    • Sei Whale

    Overview

    The sei whale, scientifically known as balaenoptera borealis, belongs to the family Balaenoptera borealis and is a type of baleen whale. Adult males and females in the North Pacific average 13.7 metres (45 feet) and 15 metres (49 feet), respectively, and weigh 15 and 18.5 tonnes (15,000-18500kg), whereas those in the North Atlantic average 14 metres (46 feet) and 14.5 metres (48 feet), respectively, and weigh 15.5 and 17 tonnes (15500-17000 kg). The sei whales have an approximate lifespan of 50-70 years.

    Diet & Habitats

    Sei whales can be found in the Atlantic, Indian, and Pacific Oceans. The sei whale typically makes an annual migration from cool, subpolar waters in the summer to temperate, subtropical areas in the winter when there is more food available. They are carnivorous and their diets consist mainly of copepods and krills or other zooplankton, depending on what is abundant in their habitat.

    Threats & Diseases

    The Sei whales are classified as endangered on the IUCN red list with an estimated population of 57,000 to 65,000. This species of whale was highly threatened due to increased poaching and hunting for commercial sale before 2008, causing the population to decrease drastically. Since 1986, the International Whaling Commission (IWC) has officially halted all commercial whaling. Despite this, 50 sei whales are still killed annually by Japanese whalers under Japan's "scientific" whaling program.

    The common threats sei whales are currently facing include:

    • Vessel strikes
    • Entanglemenmt
    • Water pollution

    Fun Facts!

    • A single sei whale can eat almost 1000kg of food in a single day
    • Sei whales have 2 blowholes instead of 1.
    • Sei whales are one of the fastest whales, reaching speeds of 55 km/hr.

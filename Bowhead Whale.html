    • Bowhead Whale

    Overview

    Bowhead Whales, scientifically known as Balaena Mysticetus, belong to the Balaenidae family. On average, they can grow up to 18.3m (60 ft) and weigh more than 54,500kg (120,000 pounds). In addition, research suggests that the bowhead whale is the longest-living mammal in the world with a lifespan of 200+ years!

    Diet and Habitat

    Bowhead whales are one of the few remaining whale species that live almost exclusively in the Arctic and subarctic waters. Due to their cold habitat, these whales have the thickest layer of blubber (43-48cm) compared to all whale species. Additionally, the bowhead whales are capable of breaking through thick ice of approximately 17cm (7 inches) using their strong and thick skull and body.
    Bowhead whales are baleen whales, this means they have fine, comb-like plates (instead of teeth) that can filter food as they swim with their mouths open. Their diet consists mostly of zooplankton and marine invertebrates such as krill. In fact, scientists believe that bowhead whales can consume over 220,000 pounds (99,790kg) of crustaceans per year.

    Threats & Diseases

    Currently, bowhead whales are classified as endangered on the IUCN Red List with a population of approximately 10,000. Throughout the 1800s to early 1900s, severe commercial whaling for bowheads off Alaska has caused their populations to decrease from 50,000 to less than 3,000. Fortunately, new conservation efforts have enabled the population to increase again, however, despite this, bowhead whales are still facing various threats. These include but are not limited to:

    • Vessel strikes and disturbance
    • Pollution
    • Global warming
    • Fishing gear entanglement
    • Ocean acidification
    • Noise pollution (affecting feeding/navigation)
    Moreover, in addition to their incredibly long lifespans, bowhead whales remain relatively disease free. It is unknown how, but these whales have mechanisms towards the resistance to age-related diseases and cancerous tumours.

    Fun Facts:

    • Their heads are 1/3 the size of their body.
    • They can blow water 6 metres (20 ft) in the air.
    • Bowheads have the largest mouth of any animal.

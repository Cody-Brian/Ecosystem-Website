    • Bees

    Overview

    There are over 20,000 species of bees in the world and are classified into seven families: Apidae, Halictidae, Megachilidae, Andrenidae, Colletidae, Melittidae, and Stenotritidae. The smallest bee species is the perdita minima (2mm) and the largest bee species is the wallace's giant bee (60mm). Bees have four wings and can communicate with each other by “dancing”.

    Diet & Habitats

    Bees feed on the pollen and nectar of plants; the nectar is for energy and the pollen is for providing protein and/or other nutrients. While honey bees live in hives above ground, 70% of bees actually live and lay eggs in underground nests. In addition, common natural habitats for bees include grasslands, prairies, etc.

    Threats & Diseases

    There are numerous threats that bees have been facing. In fact, “at least 45% of Europe's bumblebees are in population decline due to habitat fragmentation and loss of foraging plants.” (Threats) The following are some of the most common threats to bees:

    • Climate change
    • Habitat loss and fragmentation
    • Invasive plants and bees
    • Low genetic diversity
    • Pathogens spread by commercially managed bees
    • Pesticides

    In addition, there are various diseases that bees can get which threatens their health. Some of these include, Acarapisosis, American and European foulbrood, Small hive beetle infestation (Aethina tumida), and many more. (“Diseases of Bees - WOAH - World Organisation for Animal Health”)

    Fun Facts!

    • “Bees have 2 stomachs – one for eating, and one for storing nectar and processing it into honey.” (Rutter and Scott)
    • A queen bee can lay 2000 eggs per day and lay around 800,000 eggs in their lifetimes!
    • Bees have 5 eyes.

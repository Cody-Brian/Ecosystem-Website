    Overview

    Chickens are a type of small farm animals (birds). They are one of the most commonly domesticated animals as 25 billion of them exist worldwide. This is because one of the most widely consumed meat is chicken meat. Chicken eggs may have different colors due to different breeds, hens with white earlobes lay white eggs and hens with red earlobes lay brown eggs. They can lay only 1 egg per day. Hens are female chickens and roosters are male chickens they can't lay eggs. You can differentiate between the two by the color of the feathers. Roosters are more colorful and have red combs on their heads. Since, they live in groups they have their own leaders the oldest chicken is the leader of the group. Chickens also have a lot of similar behaviours to other animals, example, chickens groom themselves by preening (spread the skin oils with their beak to moisturise skin and feathers) like cats.

    Diet and Habitat

    They are omnivores, which means they can eat plant and meat based food.

    Threats & Diseases

    Diseases such as bird flu is prevented in chickens by keeping the water and food clean. Chicken farmers can know when a chicken is sick if it stops eating & drinking or lays less eggs. Chickens can also suffer from chicken mites which is a type of parasite that makes the skin and feathers dry. Farmers treat this the same way they would treat human hair lice. They are also healthier if they are kept in groups, fed 2-3 times a day and koop cleaned 2-5 times a week.

    Fun Facts:

    Chickens can form emotional connections as they can remember almost 100 faces. Chickens are one of the closest living beings to dinosaurs. There is no difference between white and brown eggs except color.

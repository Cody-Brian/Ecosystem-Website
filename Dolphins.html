    Overview

    Dolphins, scientifically known as delphinidae, are aquatic mammals (they are not fishes) with 42 different species. The orca (aka killer whales) is the largest species while Hector's dolphin and Franciscana are two of the smallest. Dolphins are required to oftenly resurface the water to breathe as an average dolphin can only hold their breath underwater for around 8-10 minutes. Although there are many dolphin species, the average lifespan of dolphins is around 20 to 90 years.

    Diet & Habitat

    Dolphins are usually found in tropical and temperate waters, although the orca is the only dolphins that live in the arctic and Antarctica. Some dolphin species prefer to live in coastal waters while others prefer patches of shallow water that are further out at sea. In addition, dolphins are classified as carnivores with their diet mainly consisting of a variety of fish, squid, shrimps, jellyfish and octopuses. However, their distinctive diet depends on their species and where they live.

    Threats & Diseases

    Unfortunately, the conservation status of dolphins ranges from least concern to extinction in the IUCN red list. The Yangtze river dolphins (aka Baiji) are the first dolphin species driven to extinction by human beings; it took less than 50 years for humans to wipe them out.
    Currently, the most detrimental threats to the dolphins population include:

    • Entanglement in fishing gear
    • Climate change
    • Vessel strikes
    • Water pollution/toxic contamination
    • Habitat destruction

    In addition, there are a variety of diseases and parasites that dolphins may acquire that can cause death. Dolphins may suffer from viral, bacterial, and fungal infections and may develop stomach ulcers, skin diseases, tumours, heart disease, urogenital disorders, and respiratory disorders. Some parasites that can also affect dolphins include tapeworms, flukes, and roundworms. There are a few ways that dolphins can be treated if they obtain a disease. Dolphins in captivity may undergo surgery and be given intravenous (IV) fluids to provide medicine. Moreover, dolphins are capable of healing at fast rates, allowing injuries and wounds to be healed quickly.

    Fun Facts:

    • A group of dolphins is called a pod.
    • Dolphins sleep with only half of their brain so that one side of the brain can alert for danger.
    • Dolphins are chatty - They make a variety of sounds such as whistles, clicks, squawks, squeaks, moans, barks, groans and yelps.

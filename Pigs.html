    Overview

    Pigs are a type of big farm animals. There are many types of pigs which makes some of them have tusks. They're a friendly animal but can get scared very easily if something is too close to their face.

    Diet & Habitat

    Pigs are onmivores and should be fed 3 times a day, they need to have clean water all the time. Since pigs are big animals they need a large space to play and live in. The are very social animals and there should always be more than one pig so that they can be healthy and happy.

    Threats & Diseases

    It is very easy for pigs to get sick so to make sure they stay healthy they need regualr visits to the vet and need to have a good diet. They should also have a lot of mud to wallow in to cool-down and care for their skin.
    But they can still get sick some of these diseases and their treatments are listed below:

    • Dry scaly skin; it can be treated using moisturising oils or less numebr of baths (baths cause the skin to dry out)
    • Sunburn; indoor stalls should have complete shade and mud should be provided along with healthy amounts of water for drinking
    • Inverted eyelids; losing weight can help the excess skin and fat from the face go back to ints natural shape which will help the eyelashes not fold into the eyeball.

    Pigs also need regular brushing is necessary to remove the dead skin cells. Bathing them is also required to keep the skin clean but you shouldn't bathe them more than once a month. Depending on the species of pig you should also trim their tusks to make sure the pigs don't hurt other humans or animals around them.

    Fun Facts:

    • Pigs can smell upto 8 meters underground.
    • They are smarter than dogs.

    • Guinea Pigs

    Overview

    Guinea pigs are a type of small domesticated pets. They are generally very anxious abou their surroundings and hence, are very alert. It is better to keep them in larger groups as they are social animals but make sure to not pick them or play with them too often as they value their personal space.

    Diet and Habitat

    They should be fed at least 3 times a day and their water should be refilled everyday with clean water. They are herbivores so always be mindful of what are you feeding them. They should be eating fruits, vegetables, berries and hay.

    Threats & Diseases

    Guinea pigs can develop diseases such as bumble foot; this is when their feet swell up and are sore. This can happen due to being overweight, cuts or infections in the body. They can also fall sick if they have a problem with their mouth and can't chew or if they are not being taken care of properly after surgery. It is also immperetive that the nails are cut regularly and fur brushed to prevent rashes or problems with walking.

    Fun Facts:

    Guinea pigs jump when they are excited this is called 'popcorning'.
